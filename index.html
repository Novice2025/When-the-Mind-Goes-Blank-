When the Mind Goes Blank

A curated list of phrases and strategies to professionally handle questions when you need time to check, research, or delegate the answer.

Series 1: Immediate Follow-Up (Checking & Reverting)

Securing Time

  • Let me check on that / Let me check that. [Deixe-me verificar isso.] *Sorry mate, I am not 100% sure of that. **Let me check on that and revert to you**.*
  • Let me run a check on that. [Deixe-me fazer uma verificação rápida nisso.] *I am afraid, **let me run a quick check on your machine to diagnose the problem**.*
  • I’ll get back to you on that one. [Eu te retorno sobre isso.] ***I’ll get back to you on the new sales proposal**.*
  • I’ll double check and let you know. [Vou verificar duas vezes e te informo.] ***I’ll double check and let you know**. Could you wait?*

Commitment to Accuracy

  • I’ll find out and let you know. [Vou descobrir e te aviso.] ***It falls beyond my duty but I will find out and let you know**.*
  • Let me find out for you. [Deixe-me descobrir por você.] ***I don't have this answer for now. Let me find out for you**.*
  • I can find out for you. [Posso descobrir para você.] ***If you give me some time, I can find out for you**.*
  • I want to be sure and give you the correct information. Let me check. [Quero ter certeza de te dar a informação correta.] *(Use this concise line to emphasize accuracy over speed.)*

Series 2: Research & Investigation (Formal & Measured)

Acknowledging Depth

  • That requires a bit more research first. [Isso exige um pouco mais de pesquisa.] ***I can't really answer this question as this requires a bit more research first** to come to a better conclusion.*
  • I’m going to investigate that further / I’m going to look into that matter further. [Vou investigar isso mais a fundo.] ***I can't really answer this question as I am going to investigate that further**.*

Valuing the Inquiry

  • That’s a really good point / question, I’ll check / I’ll find out. [Ótimo ponto, vou verificar.] ***I shall be honest here that this' really a good point however, I will check and come back to you later**.*
  • Let me look that up for you. [Deixe-me pesquisar isso para você.] ***Mum, do you understand what is < rhetorical >? No, **let me look that up for you**.*

Series 3: Transferring/Limiting Scope (Boundaries)

Setting the Scope

  • I haven’t looked at that yet. [Ainda não olhei para isso.] ***Teacher, do you know why we make terrible mistakes? Sorry, but I haven't looked at that yet**.*
  • I don’t have that information here right now. [Não tenho essa informação aqui agora.] *(Used to explain the physical absence of data without suggesting ignorance.)*
  • I haven’t had time to think that through yet. [Ainda não tive tempo de pensar nisso.] *(Focuses on time management, not capability.)*

Deflection and Delegation

  • That’s not my area of expertise, I’ll ask to (...). [Essa não é minha área, vou perguntar para...] *(**That’s not my area of expertise**, I’ll ask to **Sarah in Finance**.)*
  • I’m probably not the best / right person to ask for that information. [Eu provavelmente não sou a melhor pessoa para perguntar.] *(A polite way to redirect the inquiry.)*
  • There are several possible answers, I’ll need more information first. [Há várias respostas possíveis, preciso de mais informação.] *(Used when the question is too broad or ambiguous.)*
  • It could be one of many possibilities, I’ll look into it. [Pode ser uma de muitas possibilidades, vou investigar.] *(Similar to the above, used when diagnosis is required.)*

Series 4: Informal & Casual Admissions

Casual Honesty

  • I’m not 100% sure on that / I am not sure on that. [Não tenho 100% de certeza disso.] ***Sorry, I can't make 100% of this**. Perhaps, Science will evolve further and your question may be answered over time.*
  • I can’t remember off the top of my head. I’ll get back to you on that. [Não me lembro de cabeça.] *(Used to acknowledge the information is known but inaccessible immediately.)*
  • I have been wondering the same thing! [Eu também tenho me perguntado isso!] *(A friendly way to show empathy while admitting the unknown.)*
  • Your guess is as good as mine. [Seu palpite é tão bom quanto o meu.] ***Wow! I think that your guess is as good as mine** when you presented the facts.*

Very Informal (Use with Caution)

  • I haven’t a clue / I haven’t got a clue. [Não tenho a menor ideia.] ***Sorry everyone, I haven't the faintest idea**.*
  • Beats me / It beats me. [Me supera / Não faço ideia.] ***It beats me** how she got the job.*
  • Who knows? (Rhetorical question). [Quem sabe?] ***God was a man. Who knows?** (Rhetorical = speech or writing intended to be effective and influence people.)*
  • Take a guess. [Arrisque um palpite.] ***I rather take a guess here** as the sales trend is fluctuated every day.*

Most Professional Responses

Use these phrases to maintain credibility while delaying a response:

Practice: Handling a Tough Client Question

FINAL WORD | The Power of Diplomacy

A professional never says "I don't know." They say "I will find out." Use these phrases to demonstrate competence, diligence, and accountability.
